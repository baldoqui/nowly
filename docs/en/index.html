Nagasaki Commemorates 80th Anniversary of Atomic Bombing, Calls for Nuclear Abolition

Nagasaki, Japan, marked the 80th anniversary of the atomic bombing on August 9, 1945, with a memorial event attended by approximately 2,600 people, including representatives from over 90 countries. The ceremony at Nagasaki Peace Park featured speeches by Mayor Shiro Suzuki and Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba, a moment of silence at the exact time of the bombing, and the release of doves symbolizing peace. Survivors and their families gathered despite rainy weather to honor victims and advocate for a world free of nuclear weapons.

Germany Suspends Military Exports to Israel Amid Gaza Offensive

Germany announced it will halt authorization of military equipment exports to Israel that could be used in Gaza "until further notice," following Israel’s Cabinet decision to intensify its military operations and take over Gaza City. Chancellor Friedrich Merz stated that the escalation complicates Germany’s ability to support Israel’s stated goals of hostage release and ceasefire negotiations. While affirming Israel’s right to self-defense, Germany emphasized the need for humanitarian access and cautioned against further annexation steps in the West Bank.

U.S. and Industry Groups Submit Comments on Regulatory Proposals

Several U.S.-based organizations have recently submitted public comment letters to federal agencies regarding ongoing regulatory proposals:

  • The International Consortium for Innovation and Quality in Pharmaceutical Development urged the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to maintain the use of titanium dioxide in pharmaceutical products, emphasizing its importance for quality and innovation.

  • The American Association of Pro-Life Obstetricians and Gynecologists (AAPLOG) opposed proposed changes to mifepristone regulations by the FDA, citing patient safety concerns.

  • The Washington State Utilities and Transportation Commission submitted comments opposing a Department of Transportation proposal related to railroad crossings for commercial vehicles, highlighting safety risks.

  • The National Bobwhite and Grassland Initiative Foundation advocated for prioritizing wildlife habitat in conservation practice standards submitted to the U.S. Department of Agriculture.

  • The Insured Retirement Institute expressed concerns about a Department of Labor rule that would eliminate the definition of "plan assets," emphasizing the need for continued protections.

  • Feeding Northeast Florida and United Way of Northwest Vermont opposed a proposed Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) database, citing concerns over privacy and program integrity.

  • The National Mining Association supported Mine Safety and Health Administration proposals to remove outdated safety lamp regulations.

  • The Provenance Chain Network called for reforms in U.S. defense procurement to enhance innovation and domestic supply chains.

U.S. Transportation and Safety Groups Respond to Federal Regulatory Proposals

Several transportation and safety organizations in the United States have voiced their positions on recent federal regulatory proposals:

  • The American Trucking Associations supported the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration’s (FMCSA) proposal to remove outdated flare regulations, aiming to modernize safety standards.

  • The American Bus Association opposed a proposed FMCSA exemption related to railroad grade crossings, citing potential safety risks for commercial vehicles.

  • The Truck Safety Coalition, Citizens for Reliable and Safe Highways, and Parents Against Tired Truckers jointly opposed FMCSA’s proposed change in the definition of "medical treatment," emphasizing implications for driver safety.

  • The Virginia Department of Rail and Public Transportation submitted concerns over new safety standards announced by the Department of Transportation, highlighting operational impacts.
