US-Brokered Peace Deal Signed by Armenia and Azerbaijan at White House

Armenia and Azerbaijan have signed a significant peace agreement at a White House summit facilitated by US President Donald Trump. The deal aims to end decades of conflict between the two South Caucasus nations, reopening key transportation routes and establishing a major transit corridor dubbed the "Trump Route for International Peace and Prosperity." This corridor is expected to enhance economic ties and expand US influence in the region, particularly as Russia’s role diminishes.

The agreement includes commitments to cease hostilities, normalize diplomatic relations, and respect territorial integrity. Both leaders praised Trump for his role in brokering the deal and indicated plans to nominate him for a Nobel Peace Prize. The peace accord also lifts restrictions on defense cooperation between Azerbaijan and the United States, a move that could further strain Moscow’s influence. However, analysts caution that the long-term success of the agreement depends on sustained US engagement to prevent renewed tensions.

Israel Plans Gradual Takeover of Gaza City Amid Rising Civilian Concerns

Israel announced plans to intensify its military campaign against Hamas by gradually taking control of Gaza City. The operation, requiring mobilization of thousands of troops and likely forcible evacuation of civilians, is expected to exacerbate the ongoing humanitarian crisis in the territory. The timing of the operation remains unspecified. Hamas dismissed the plan, warning against expanded aggression.

Mediators from Egypt and Qatar are working on a framework proposing a simultaneous release of all hostages held by Hamas in exchange for cessation of hostilities and Israeli withdrawal. The plan includes a transitional Palestinian-Arab committee to govern Gaza and oversee reconstruction efforts. International powers, including Germany, have criticized the escalation; Germany announced it will halt military exports to Israel that could be used in Gaza until further notice.

Germany Suspends Military Exports to Israel Over Gaza Offensive

In a swift response to Israel's decision to take over Gaza City, German Chancellor Friedrich Merz announced that Germany will not authorize any exports of military equipment to Israel that could be used in Gaza “until further notice.” This decision marks a significant shift by one of Israel’s strongest European allies and adds to growing international criticism of the nearly two-year conflict.

Merz affirmed Israel’s right to self-defense against Hamas but underscored the need for humanitarian access and a ceasefire. The German government expressed concern over the escalating military action and its impact on civilians. The suspension affects equipment supplied by German firms, which constitute about 30% of Israel’s defense imports, especially naval armaments. Other European nations, including the Netherlands, Denmark, and Slovenia, have also taken or proposed measures limiting arms exports to Israel amid the conflict.

President Trump to Meet Russian President Putin in Alaska to Discuss Ukraine War

US President Donald Trump announced he will meet Russian President Vladimir Putin on August 15 in Alaska to discuss ending the war in Ukraine. This high-profile summit represents a potential breakthrough after months of stalled diplomatic efforts and ongoing conflict that has lasted over three years.

Trump indicated that a peace deal might involve territorial exchanges between Russia and Ukraine but provided no detailed proposals. He emphasized that the meeting would precede any discussions involving Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy, raising concerns about Ukraine’s role in peace negotiations. The Kremlin has yet to confirm the meeting details. Analysts note that despite the summit, significant differences remain between Moscow and Kyiv, making a lasting peace agreement uncertain.

US Justice Department Intensifies Investigations into Trump Critics

The US Justice Department has escalated scrutiny of prominent critics of President Donald Trump, including New York Attorney General Letitia James and Senator Adam Schiff. Subpoenas have been issued seeking records related to lawsuits and investigations involving alleged fraud and mortgage fraud.

Special prosecutors have been appointed to assist in these investigations. Both James and Schiff deny wrongdoing and characterize the probes as politically motivated. These actions represent some of the most serious legal challenges faced by Trump’s opponents and reflect ongoing political tensions within the US.

Major League Baseball: Dodgers, Astros, and Other Teams Secure Key Wins

In Major League Baseball action, the Los Angeles Dodgers defeated the Toronto Blue Jays 5-1, with Clayton Kershaw earning the win and Mookie Betts hitting a pivotal two-run homer. The Houston Astros triumphed over the New York Yankees 5-3 in extra innings, highlighted by Carlos Correa’s leadoff single and Taylor Trammell’s two-run homer.

Other notable results include the Detroit Tigers rallying to beat the Los Angeles Angels 6-5, the Pittsburgh Pirates overcoming the Cincinnati Reds 3-2, and the Cleveland Guardians securing a 9-5 victory over the Chicago White Sox. The Boston Red Sox shut out the San Diego Padres 10-2, while the Seattle Mariners extended their winning streak to five with a 3-2 win against the Tampa Bay Rays. The Milwaukee Brewers continued their hot streak with a 3-2 comeback win over the New York Mets.

These games reflect intense competition as teams approach the critical stages of the MLB season.
