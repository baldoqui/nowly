U.S. Special Forces at Fort Bragg Linked to Drug Trafficking and Murders

A new investigative report reveals that Fort Bragg, the largest U.S. military base and home to elite units such as Delta Force and the 82nd Airborne Division, is embroiled in narcotics trafficking and associated violence. Author Seth Harp, in his book The Fort Bragg Cartel: Drug Trafficking and Murder in the Special Forces, details at least 14 cases over the past five years involving soldiers trained at Fort Bragg who were arrested, killed, or implicated in drug trafficking, often linked to Mexican cartels like Los Zetas.

The book highlights the discovery of two murdered soldiers in December 2020 on a Fort Bragg training range, suspected victims of a drug deal gone wrong. Harp describes how Los Zetas, a cartel originally trained by U.S. Special Forces as an elite Mexican paratrooper unit, later turned rogue and became heavily involved in drug trafficking. The report also notes over 100 soldier deaths at Fort Bragg from 2020 to 2021, mostly stateside, with many linked to suicide and drug-related causes. Despite the severity, no significant investigations or reforms have been initiated.

Harp critiques the broader U.S. military and government complicity in narcotics, pointing to Afghanistan’s status as the largest heroin producer during the U.S. occupation, with drug trafficking facilitated by warlords and officials allied with U.S. forces. The book also exposes covert U.S. Special Forces operations in countries like Ukraine and Mexico, often without public or congressional oversight. The report raises concerns about recent Pentagon directives authorizing military action against drug cartels abroad, questioning the legality and effectiveness of such measures.

Kaiser Permanente to Pause Gender-Affirming Surgeries for Under-19 Patients

Kaiser Permanente announced it will halt gender-affirming surgeries for patients under the age of 19 starting August 29, citing threats of federal funding cuts amid ongoing political and legal pressures. The decision follows similar service reductions by Stanford Medicine and Children’s Hospital Los Angeles. Kaiser officials emphasized that non-surgical gender-affirming care, such as hormone therapy and counseling, will continue.

The pause responds to executive orders and federal actions initiated under the Trump administration, which labeled gender-affirming care for minors as harmful and sought to restrict it nationwide. California law prohibits denying services based on gender identity, but the threat of losing federal funds has prompted providers to curtail services. Advocacy groups warn that such restrictions increase risks of anxiety, depression, and suicide among transgender youth.

Protests have occurred outside Kaiser’s Los Angeles Medical Center, with supporters demanding continued access to care, while some opponents celebrate the decision. Kaiser remains recognized for LGBTQ+ healthcare but faces challenges balancing legal compliance and patient needs amid a politically charged environment.

G20 Interfaith Forum in Cape Town Emphasizes Ubuntu Philosophy for Global Challenges

The 2025 G20 Interfaith Forum convened in Cape Town, South Africa, under the theme “Ubuntu in Action,” bringing together over 500 global leaders to address pressing world issues through the African philosophy of Ubuntu, meaning “I am because we are.” Leaders from The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints representing various African regions highlighted themes of compassion, community resilience, and personal responsibility.

Speakers emphasized practical compassion, self-reliance, and accountability to foster stronger, more just societies. Discussions included religious freedom, humanitarian aid, and the importance of leading with respect and love beginning within families. The forum underscored the interconnectedness of humanity and the role of faith communities in promoting sustainable development and social equity.

Participants expressed optimism that the forum’s outcomes would bring transformative change, particularly across the African continent, reinforcing the spirit of unity and collective action.

Legendary Liverpool Soap "Brookside" to Return in Special Crossover Episode

The iconic Liverpool-based soap opera Brookside, which originally aired from 1982 to 2003, is set to make a special return in October 2025 as part of a crossover episode with its successor, Hollyoaks, celebrating the latter’s 30th anniversary. The episode will revisit well-known characters and storylines, focusing on the Corkhill family, one of the show’s most memorable households.

Key actors, including John McArdle (Billy Corkhill), Kate Fitzgerald (Doreen Corkhill), Jason Hope (Rod Corkhill), and Dean Sullivan (Jimmy Corkhill), have had varied careers post-Brookside, from stage performances to television roles. The crossover aims to honor the show’s legacy and reconnect fans with beloved characters after over two decades off-air.

The episode is expected to evoke nostalgia while introducing Brookside’s influential storytelling to new audiences.

The Grateful Dead’s “Dark Star” Celebrated in Retrospective of Best Performances

Rolling Stone commemorates the Grateful Dead’s seminal song “Dark Star” with an in-depth look at the band’s 20 best live versions spanning from 1967 to 1994. The song, emblematic of the band’s improvisational ethos, evolved from a short single to an expansive, freeform jam vehicle that defined the Dead’s concerts.

Highlights include the 1969 Fillmore West version that became the definitive live recording, the 1972 Europe tour performances showcasing intense experimental interplay, and the 1989 Miami rendition featuring innovative MIDI effects. Each rendition reflects the band’s willingness to explore new musical territories, with “Dark Star” serving as a living, evolving composition.

The retrospective underscores “Dark Star”’s enduring influence on psychedelic rock and improvisational music, celebrating its legacy as a cornerstone of the Grateful Dead’s artistry.

Midwest Faces Emergency Power Shortage as MISO Seeks Additional Generation

The Midcontinent Independent System Operator (MISO), responsible for managing the electric grid across much of the U.S. Midwest, is confronting a critical shortage of electric power generation. In response, the U.S. Secretary of Energy has ordered additional generation capacity to address the emergency.

MISO’s situation raises concerns about grid reliability and energy security in the region amid rising demand and potential supply constraints. The agency has communicated these challenges in a public comment letter to the U.S. Department of Energy, seeking support and coordination to mitigate the shortage.

This development highlights ongoing pressures on the U.S. energy infrastructure and the need for strategic planning to ensure continuous power availability.
